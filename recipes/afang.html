Description

Afang Soup is a rich and nutritious traditional delicacy from the Efik and Ibibio people of Southern Nigeria. It is a vegetable soup made with a blend of okazi (afang) leaves and waterleaf, combined with meat, fish, and seasonings.

This soup is popular for its unique flavor and health benefits. It is usually enjoyed with fufu, garri, or pounded yam, making it a staple dish for celebrations and everyday family meals.

Ingredients

  • Grinded okazi (afang) leaves
  • Waterleaf
  • Assorted meat (beef, tripe, cow skin)
  • Stockfish (dried fish)
  • Smoked fish
  • Periwinkle (optional)
  • Palm oil
  • Onions (optional)
  • Peppers
  • Crayfish
  • Salt
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Water
  • Dry fish

Steps

  1. Wash and slice the waterleaf and Afang leaves separately. Pound or blend the Afang leaves until finely ground.
  2. Boil the meat, stockfish, and dry fish with seasoning cubes and salt until tender.
  3. Add palm oil, crayfish, and pepper to the pot. Allow it to simmer for a few minutes.
  4. Add the waterleaf and let it cook until it releases some water.

  6. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve hot with fufu, garri, or pounded yam.
